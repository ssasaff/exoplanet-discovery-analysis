NASA Exoplanet Discoveries 1992-2024

Salsabil Assaf November 12, 2024

This chart shows the dramatic increase in exoplanet discoveries from 1992 to 2024, highlighting the impact of advanced space missions and technology. After 2009, when NASA launched the Kepler mission, the rate of discoveries increased, marking a turning point in our search for planets beyond our solar system.This data is sourced from NASA's Exoplanet Archive.

Data source: NASA Exoplanet Archive, California Institute of Technology. "Confirmed Exoplanets Table." Accessed from https://exoplanetarchive.ipac.caltech.edu/cgi-bin/TblView/nph-tblView?app=ExoTbls&config=PS.

Note: This chart was created by filtering the data by the year each exoplanet was discovered and its name. The filtered dataset was then used to count the number of exoplanets discovered each year, highlighting trends in discovery rates over time.
