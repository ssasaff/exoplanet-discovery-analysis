NASA's Exoplanet Discoveries Surge After 2009 Kepler Mission Launch

Salsabil Assaf November 12, 2024

While NASA's Kepler mission launched in 2009, the most significant discoveries came in 2014 and 2016 due to breakthroughs in data analysis and verification, resulting in record-setting numbers of confirmed exoplanets. 

Note: This chart illustrates the annual count of confirmed exoplanets discovered by NASA from 1992 to 2024. The data, sourced from NASA's Exoplanet Archive, was filtered by discovery year and name to show the growth in discoveries over time. The Kepler mission, launched in 2009, significantly increased detection rates, with record peaks in 2014 and 2016 due to cumulative data analysis and verification efforts.

Source: NASA Exoplanet Archive, California Institute of Technology. "Confirmed Exoplanets Table." Accessed from https://exoplanetarchive.ipac.caltech.edu/cgi-bin/TblView/nph-tblView?app=ExoTbls&config=PS.
